In the past 2 decades, serious mosquito and tickborne infections, such as Lyme borreliosis and West Nile virus infection, have emerged in suburban settings throughout the United States [1, 2]. Fleaborne diseases, such as epidemic and endemic (murine) typhus, which had previously been common in high-density urban human and rodent populations, have nearly been eliminated in the United States [3–7].
