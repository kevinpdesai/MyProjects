Malaria is endemic in India and constitutes a major public health challenge. An estimated 2–3 million malaria cases resulting in 500–800 deaths are reported annually nationwide, with 95% of India's population at risk of infection.1,2 As has been demonstrated in sub-Saharan Africa and Thailand,2–5 the few available epidemiologic studies of malaria in pregnancy (MIP) in India found that malaria was associated with adverse maternal and neonatal outcomes including maternal anemia, preterm labor, stillbirths, and low birth weight
