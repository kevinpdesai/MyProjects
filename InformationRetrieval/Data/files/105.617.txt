Doxycycline is typically used first line, although some strains of V. cholerae have shown resistance.[11] Testing for resistance during an outbreak can help determine appropriate future choices.[11] Other antibiotics proven to be effective include cotrimoxazole, erythromycin, tetracycline, chloramphenicol, and furazolidone.[44] Fluoroquinolones, such as ciprofloxacin, also may be used, but resistance has been reported
