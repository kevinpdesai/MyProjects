Isolated Ebola cases continue to surface in the U.S. or among American aid workers in West Africa and questions still remain over just what best practices should be applied; even on Monday, health officials tweaked protocols for health workers returning from West Africa. Because there is no scientifically proven treatment or vaccine available for patients, health officials are left to make exceptions for drugs that show promise but that have not undergone a formal review process that would determine how safe or effective they are.
