India's government has initiated measures to improve malaria prevention and treatment, including among pregnant women, although actual implementation has been inconsistent. Some states have benefited from the Enhanced Malaria Control Project, funded by the World Bank, in which vector control using larvicides and indoor residual spraying (IRS), distribution of insecticide-treated bed nets (ITNs), and early case detection and treatment of malaria with chloroquine (pregnant women) plus primaquine (non-pregnant women) have been used.11,12 In some districts, a comprehensive malaria strategy exists on paper, but control measures are limited in availability or practice
