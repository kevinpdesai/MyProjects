On 25 March 2014, the World Health Organization (WHO) reported that Guinea's Ministry of Health had reported an outbreak of Ebola virus disease in four southeastern districts, with suspected cases in the neighbouring countries of Liberia and Sierra Leone being investigated. In Guinea, a total of 86 suspected cases, including 59 deaths had been reported as of 24 March.[42] By late May, the outbreak had spread to Conakry, Guinea's capital, a city of about two million inhabitants.[42] On 28 May, the total number of cases reported had reached 281 with 186 deaths.[42]
