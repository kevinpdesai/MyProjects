March 2014
24th: Authorities in Guinea and the WHO said the country had recorded 87 suspected cases of viral haemorrhagic fever since January, including 61 deaths. Scientists studied samples in the French city of Lyon and confirmed the disease as Ebola.
31st: Liberia confirmed its first two cases of the virus; the first suspected cases were also reported in Sierra Leone. Volunteer aid organisations began flocking to the region to determine the true extent.
