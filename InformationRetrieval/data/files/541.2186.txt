Three common types of Typhus are known:
Epidemic Typhus: It is the most common type of Typhus and if not specified, the term Typhus refers to Epidemic Typhus. It spreads from one person to another by human lice.
Murine Typhus:  It is a milder form of Typhus which is usually spread by rat flea or cat flea.
Scrub Typhus: It is also a mild form. It spreads by the bite of a mite and is characterized by the presence of eschar. Eschar means a black colored, painless skin lesion.
