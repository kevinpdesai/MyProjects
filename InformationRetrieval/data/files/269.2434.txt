 How to prevent the spread of disease Communities can take the following steps to reduce the transmission of cholera The stools of people infected with Vibrio cholerae 01 contain the cholera organism even if they themselves are not sick. If cholera germs from these stools contaminate food or water, they can be swallowed by other people, which spreads the disease. This process is called faecal-oral transmission.
